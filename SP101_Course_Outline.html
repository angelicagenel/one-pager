Unit 1 — Hola, soy yo

SER · tú/usted · Hay · Preguntas integradas · Identity & First Contact
📖 Cuéntame: La Mulata de Córdoba (Veracruz colonial, s.XVII)
🎨 Cultural Theme: Confianza — the greeting as relational act
I can introduce myself and ask others about their name, origin, and occupation. (Interpersonal) · I can understand a short introduction in Spanish. (Interpretive Listening) · I can describe what exists in a space. (Presentational Speaking) · I can recognize Mexican greeting practices. (Cultures — Practices)
L01 Mucho gusto, soy yo Focal: SER (soy/eres/es/somos/son) ✅ Build Complete ▾
Communicative Situation
First meeting at the feria in Oaxaca — introducing yourself, saying your name, where you're from, and what you do. Two scenes: singular forms (soy/eres/es) → plural forms (somos/son) when don Marcos introduces his friends.
Can-Do Statements
1. I can understand a short introduction in Spanish. (Interpretive Listening)
2. I can introduce myself and describe a group using SER. (Presentational Speaking)
3. I can ask someone their name, where they're from, and who others are. (Interpersonal)
Focal Structure
SER — complete paradigm: soy / eres / es / somos / son
tú/usted as pragmatic register marker (NOT a separate grammar paradigm — enters Paso a Paso as functional vocabulary)
Connectors: y, también modeled in input, practiced in writing
Vocabulary Box (Flashcards — 6 items)
me llamomucho gusto¿De dónde eres?buenos díasvendedor/acafé de olla
SER forms enter exclusively through Guided Noticing + Paso a Paso, not Vocabulary Preview.
Preguntas Integradas
Desglosada: ¿Cómo te llamas? (informal) vs. ¿Cómo se llama usted? (formal) — TE vs. SE signals register.
Quick Tip: ¿A qué se dedica? = "What do you dedicate yourself to?" — more respectful than ¿Qué haces?
Practice: Match question to situation · Build question from word bank
SKILL 4 — Writing
Connectors: y, también
Modeled in input. Writing task: "Write 2 sentences about yourself. Use 'y' to connect."
SKILL 6 — Listening & Speaking
Repair: ¿Cómo? ¿Puede repetir? Más despacio, por favor.
Pronunciation: Spanish 5-vowel system vs. English ~15 vowels.
First meeting at the feria. Mexican greeting practices: beso, abrazo, handshake. "Mucho gusto" as relational, not formulaic. Connects to Unit Cultural Theme: confianza.
Legend connection: Warm-up references Mesoamerican naming tradition. Full legend appears in CT1.
L02 ¿Qué hay aquí? Focal: hay (there is/are) — invariable chunk ⬜ Pending ▾
Communicative Situation
Describing what's at a place — Don Marcos describes his puesto at the feria to doña Rosa and the students.
Can-Do Statements
1. I can understand a description of what's available at a place. (Interpretive Listening)
2. I can describe what exists in a space using hay. (Presentational Speaking)
Focal Structure
hay — formulaic chunk, invariable (there is / there are). No Grammar Block — enters Vocabulary Box as chunk.
Recycling: SER (L01) ♻️ · tú/usted ♻️
Vocabulary Box (14 items)
CORE: hayno haycafé de ollatamalesagua de jamaicapanmercadopuesto SUPPORT: feriapersonasmucho/poco CHUNKS: Hay café de olla.¿Qué hay en ___?No hay ___, pero hay ___.
Preguntas Integradas
Desglosada: Questions with "hay" use intonation only: "¿Hay café?" vs. "Hay café." Rising intonation = question.
Quick Tip: ¿Qué hay? is a common greeting in Mexico (like "What's up?").
Practice: Match question to context · True/False (hay vs. no hay)
SKILL 4 — Writing
Connector: pero
Modeled: "No hay pan, pero hay tamales." Writing task: "Write 2 sentences about your school. Use 'pero.'
SKILL 6 — Listening & Speaking
Rehearsed formulas — "Hay ___" as a single-chunk answer to any "what's there?" question. Building confidence through repetition.
Don Marcos's puesto at the feria — what's available, what's not, what makes each vendor unique. The feria as community space.

Cognate strategy — recognizing English-Spanish cognates: el hospital, la universidad, el restaurante. Cognates as confidence builders.

L04 Los tamales, las personas Focal: Plural articles (los/las) + noun pluralization ⬜ Pending ▾
Communicative Situation
Talking about multiple things and people at the feria. Plurality as community.
Can-Do Statements
1. I can use plural articles and pluralize nouns. (Presentational Speaking)
2. I can ask and answer questions about quantity. (Interpersonal)
Focal Structure
Plural articles: los / las / unos / unas + noun pluralization (-s / -es).
Recycling: Artículos singular (L03) ♻️ · SER (L01): "Son de Oaxaca." ♻️ · hay (L02) ♻️
Vocabulary Box (12 items)

Desglosada: ¿Cuántos? (m) vs. ¿Cuántas? (f) — question word agrees with the noun.
Quick Tip: Add -s if noun ends in vowel (mesa → mesas), -es if consonant (señor → señores).
Practice: Match singular to plural · Choose ¿Cuántos/Cuántas?
SKILL 4 — Writing
Connector: y
Writing task: "Write 2 sentences about people at the feria. Use plural forms and 'y.'
SKILL 6 — Listening & Speaking
Pattern recognition — listening for -s and -es endings to identify plural forms. Pronunciation: plural -es adds a syllable.
The feria as collective space — vendors (vendedores), people (personas), stalls (puestos). Plurality as community.

CT1 Cuéntame — La Mulata de Córdoba  ⬜ Pending

Legend
A woman accused of witchcraft in colonial Veracruz draws a ship on the wall and escapes. About identity, mystery, and freedom. Narrated by Angélica García Genel. Adapted from podcast EP01.
Can-Do Statements
I can understand an adapted legend in Spanish. (Interpretive Listening + Reading)
I can write a short reflection about identity. (Presentational Writing)
Vocabulario Reciclado

Estrategia de Lectura (3 pasos)
Paso 1: Escucha global — "Who is the main character? Where is she?"
Paso 2: Lee y escucha (SER forms highlighted)
Paso 3 (opcional): Detalle — "What does she draw? What happens?"
Yo Escribo Task
"Imagine you are the Mulata de Córdoba. Write 4-6 sentences introducing yourself. Who are you? Where are you from? What do people say about you? Use SER and articles."
📌 Register note: The governor addresses the Mulata with a command — she responds with "Yo soy libre." This is the first literary moment where register and power collide. Students are not assessed on this, but it anchors the tú/usted discussion introduced in L01.

CT5 Cuéntame — Huehuetéotl: El Anciano del Fuego  ⬜ Pending

Legend

Can-Do Statements
I can understand a legend about being lost and found. (Interpretive Listening + Reading)

Vocabulario Reciclado
ESTAR loc./emoc. (está perdido, está asustado) · IR/VENIR (va a casa, el anciano lo guía, llega) · progressive (está caminando) · la sombra ya no está
Estrategia de Lectura
Paso 1: "Who is Jacinto? What happens to him?"
Paso 2: ESTAR, IR, VENIR highlighted
Paso 3: "Who helps him? Where does the old man go?"
Yo Escribo Task
"Write about a time you felt lost or found. Where were you? How did you feel? Who helped you? Use ESTAR (location + emotion), IR, and VENIR."
📌 Register note: Jacinto speaks to the old man using usted — he is a stranger, an elder, a figure of authority. The fire god addresses him with tú. This asymmetry of register (young person → elder = usted / elder → young person = tú) is explicitly noted in the comprehension questions and cultural reflection, recycling the tú/usted distinction introduced in L01 and practiced with family members in L08. Students are asked: "Why does Jacinto use usted? When do you use usted vs. tú in your own language?"

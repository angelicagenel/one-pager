Elementary Spanish I

SP101 · 5 Units · Novice Mid/High

AVAILABLE

A Spanish learning system grounded in Mexican culture & built on cutting-edge technology.

Five units built around the Oaxacan feria and recurring characters don Marcos, doña Rosa, Tomás, Sofía, and Elena. Students go from first greetings to present progressive, grounded in Mexican culture and delivered through flipped communicative lessons.

What Students Can Do at Exit

  1. Greet, introduce themselves, and share basic personal information
  2. Describe people, places, and things using adjectives and articles
  3. Talk about daily routines and actions using present tense verbs
  4. Tell time, use numbers, and describe schedules
  5. Say what is happening right now and express simple future plans
  6. Connect ideas using basic connectors (y, pero, primero, después)

Course Sequence

Click any unit to see grammar focus and communicative goals. View full lesson outline →

Identity and first contact at the Oaxacan feria.

  • I can introduce myself and ask about others.
  • I can describe what exists in a space.

Describing people, places, and things.

  • I can describe people and objects using adjectives.
  • I can talk about what belongs to me and others.

Daily routines and actions in context.

  • I can describe my daily routine.
  • I can organize a sequence of events.

Time, schedules, and numbers in daily life.

  • I can tell time and describe my schedule.
  • I can express what I have and need.

Location, movement, emotions, and present actions.

  • I can describe where things are and how I feel.
  • I can say where I'm going and what's happening right now.

Optional extended track for full SP101 grammatical coverage.

  • I can use a wider range of verb forms.
  • I can express more complex ideas with precision.

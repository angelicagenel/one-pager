Elementary Spanish I

SP101 · 5 Units

Available

A Spanish learning system grounded in Mexican culture & built on cutting-edge technology.

Five units built around the Oaxacan feria and recurring characters don Marcos, doña Rosa, Tomás, Sofía, and Elena. Students go from first greetings to present progressive — grounded in Mexican culture, delivered through flipped communicative lessons.

What Students Can Do at Exit

  1. Greet, introduce themselves, and share basic personal information
  2. Describe people, places, and things using adjectives and articles
  3. Talk about daily routines and actions using present tense verbs
  4. Tell time, use numbers, and describe schedules
  5. Say what is happening right now and express simple future plans
  6. Connect ideas using basic connectors (y, pero, primero, después)

Course Sequence

Click any unit to see grammar focus and communicative goals.

Communicative Situation

Students introduce themselves and ask about others in a new Mexican cultural environment.

Grammar Focal Structures

SER, tú/usted, hay, articles, nouns

Can-Do Statements

  • I can introduce myself and ask about others.
  • I can describe what exists in a space.

Communicative Situation

Students describe the people, places, and things around them using adjectives and possessives.

Grammar Focal Structures

Adjectives, SER + adjective, possessives, family vocabulary

Can-Do Statements

  • I can describe people and things using adjectives.
  • I can talk about what belongs to me and others.

Communicative Situation

Students narrate daily life and sequence activities using regular verbs and connectors.

Grammar Focal Structures

Regular -AR/-ER/-IR verbs, sequence connectors

Can-Do Statements

  • I can describe my daily routine.
  • I can organize a sequence of events using connectors.

Communicative Situation

Students discuss schedules, tell time, and express what they own and need.

Grammar Focal Structures

La hora, TENER, numbers 0–100, schedules

Can-Do Statements

  • I can tell time and describe my schedule.
  • I can express what I have and need.

Communicative Situation

Students describe ongoing actions, locations, emotions, and immediate future plans.

Grammar Focal Structures

ESTAR location + emotion, IR + a + infinitive, VENIR, present progressive

Can-Do Statements

  • I can describe where things are and how I feel.
  • I can say where I'm going and what I'm doing right now.

Communicative Situation

Students extend their grammatical range with more complex verb forms and pronoun structures.

Grammar Focal Structures

Stem-changing verbs, irregular yo forms, SER/ESTAR contrast, direct object pronouns

Can-Do Statements

  • I can use a wider range of verb forms and structures.
  • I can express more complex ideas with precision.

Elementary Spanish II

SP102 · Continues from SP101 · Intermediate Low

UPCOMING

A Spanish learning system grounded in Mexican culture & built on cutting-edge technology.

Continues from Elementary Spanish I (SP101). Builds directly on present tense, basic vocabulary, and communicative foundations.

Continues from SP101. Students move into past narration, reflexive verbs, celebrations, health, and the preterite/imperfect contrast — building on SP101's communicative foundation with new cultural contexts.

What Students Can Do at Exit

  1. Narrate completed past events using the preterite
  2. Describe habitual past actions and background context using the imperfect
  3. Talk about daily routines including reflexive verbs and personal care
  4. Discuss food, celebrations, and cultural practices
  5. Express preferences, obligations, and health situations
  6. Compare people, objects, and experiences

Course Sequence

Six thematic clusters — each building on the last.

Markets, prices, and purchases in Mexican cultural context.

  • I can narrate a purchase.
  • I can explain what I bought and for whom.

Personal care, habits, and cultural routines.

  • I can describe my daily routine in detail.
  • I can talk about habits and personal care.

Food, social meals, and Mexican celebrations.

  • I can recount a celebration or meal.
  • I can express preferences and make comparisons.

Health situations and medical contexts in Mexico.

  • I can explain symptoms and health situations.
  • I can describe obligations and habits.

Telling stories and recounting experiences.

  • I can narrate a past experience with logical sequence.
  • I can distinguish main events from background context.

Describing people, clothing, and experiences in detail.

  • I can describe and compare people and experiences.
  • I can build longer sentences using relative clauses.

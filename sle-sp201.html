Intermediate Spanish I

SP201 · 5 Clusters

Upcoming

A Spanish learning system grounded in Mexican culture & built on cutting-edge technology.

Students move beyond description and narration — into persuasion, hypothesis, and cultural argument. Commands, subjunctive, future, conditional, and argumentative discourse build confident, nuanced expression.

Continues from SP102.

What Students Can Do at Exit

  1. Give instructions and recommendations using formal and informal commands
  2. Express emotions, doubts, and opinions using the subjunctive
  3. Talk about future plans and hypothetical situations
  4. Reflect on past experiences using the present perfect
  5. Argue a position using advanced discourse markers
  6. Describe and compare cultural values across communities

Course Sequence

Click any unit to see grammar focus and communicative goals.

Communicative Situation

Students give instructions and recommendations across formal and informal registers.

Grammar Focal Structures

Affirmative/negative tú commands, formal commands, por vs. para, reciprocal se

Can-Do Statements

  • I can give instructions and recommendations.
  • I can adapt my register to the social situation.

Communicative Situation

Students express emotions, doubts, and reactions using the subjunctive mood.

Grammar Focal Structures

Present subjunctive (emotion, doubt, negation), subjunctive in adjective clauses

Can-Do Statements

  • I can express emotions, doubts, and reactions using the subjunctive.
  • I can nuance my opinions grammatically.

Communicative Situation

Students discuss future plans and hypothetical scenarios using future and conditional.

Grammar Focal Structures

Simple future, conditional, imperfect subjunctive in fixed structures

Can-Do Statements

  • I can talk about future plans and hypothetical situations.
  • I can express possibility and give evidence-based recommendations.

Communicative Situation

Students reflect on accumulated experiences and construct argumentative discourse.

Grammar Focal Structures

Present perfect, nosotros commands, advanced discourse markers

Can-Do Statements

  • I can reflect on accumulated experiences.
  • I can argue a position using organized discourse.

Communicative Situation

Students argue cultural and social topics with advanced grammatical and rhetorical tools.

Grammar Focal Structures

Advanced relative pronouns, full subjunctive vs. indicative contrast, all SP201 connectors

Can-Do Statements

  • I can express well-supported opinions on social and cultural topics.
  • I can write and speak argumentatively with confidence.
